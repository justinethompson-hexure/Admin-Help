Sales Agreements

Carrier Steps for Establishing a Sales Agreement with a Distributor & Deploying a Product: This document demonstrates how a Carrier establishes a new sales agreement in Firelight between the Carrier and a Distributor or a Distributor’s sub-firm and deploy a product to the Distributor’s Firelight instance.

To go to the Sales Agreements page, on the navigation bar, click Product Config and then click Sales Agreements.

Use the Sales Agreements page to define sales agreements between product manufacturers (carriers) and product sellers or distributors. This includes the products and packages included in the sales agreement.

Guidelines:

  • Carriers create a sales agreement for each product and all selling organizations that sell the product. For example, if the Term Protect product is sold by five distributors, the carrier will create five separate Term Protect sales agreements, one for each distributor. The carrier can specify a deployment distributor email notification distro in the Deploy Email Group field when deployments occur.

  • Carriers and Main Org distributor organizations can associate sales agreements with specific Sub Firms. See the Firm Orgs field in "Add and Edit a Sales Agreement" below for information.

  • Carriers can also create sales agreements for products that are sold directly through their environment. The carrier selects their organization name for the sales agreement in the Selling Org list.

  • Distributors can optionally add their own packages of forms, wizards and rule sets to a carrier product sales agreement in their environment. For example, the distributor may wish to add a Suitability wizard to all annuity products. The distributor selects the carrier for the sales agreement in the Carrier Org list to view and add distributor packages to an existing sales agreement for the selected carrier. The carrier sales agreement now comprises the carrier product plus any additional forms and wizards from the distributor.

  • Distributors can also create their own sales agreements. This typically applies to pre-sale activities such as a client questionnaire. The distributor selects their organization name for the sales agreement in the Carrier Org list to view and add packages to sales agreements.

  • Sales agreements and the products, packages and custom lists, plus forms, wizards, and rule sets within the sales agreements need to be deployed to the applicable environments for the carriers and distributors. See the All Configuration descriptions in Deploy Data for guidelines and detailed instructions for deploying the applicable deployment scope for carrier and distributor environments.
  • Distributors can select Auto Deploy to automatically deploy changes from a carrier configuration to the distributor configuration. See the Auto Deploy description below for details.
  • Distributors can select Allow Carrier Testing to allow carriers to test their own carrier products and activities in the distributor environment.
  • Sales agreement history appears on the Sales Agreement History tab of the Sales Agreements page.
  • Manage Sales Agreements feature allows carriers to manage Sales Agreements for multiple Selling Organizations, Copy Agreement, Delete Agreement, Save Batch, and Delete Batch. This option enables admin users to save the batches of organizations to use in the future for quick management of multiple Sales Agreements.

Following are tasks you can accomplish on the Sales Agreements page.

Add and Edit Sales Agreements

  2. Select a product in the Product Agreement grid. You can search for a product by typing search text in the text box in the upper left of the screen and then clicking the magnifying glass search icon.

  3. Below the Product grid, add/edit and view the sales agreement information.

  4. Click the Save Sales Agreement button to save the information.

  5. Grant access to the new product in Organization Configuration/Groups on the Product Access tab.

    • Carriers select the carrier products for each applicable carrier role code in their own carrier environment.
    • Distributors select a carrier in the Organization list and select the carrier products for each applicable distributor role code in their distributor environment.

  6. Deploy the Group Configuration and the applicable All Configuration scopes to the applicable environments in Utilities/Deployments (see Deploy Data for guidelines and instructions).

    • Carriers and distributors use the Group Configuration Synchronization Scope to deploy all changes to the role codes modified through Groups in Organization Configuration.
    • Carriers use the All Configuration in Synchronization Scope to deploy the top level of data (products/packages/forms/rule sets/sales agreements, etc.) modified in Product Configuration to the next carrier environment, and they use the Distributor All Configuration to deploy their products/packages/forms/rule sets/sales agreements, etc., to distributors.
    • Distributors use the All Configuration in Synchronization Scope to deploy the distributor data, including Sales Agreements, to the next distributor environment and to deploy carrier data to their next distributor environments, including Sales Agreements, when not using Auto Deploy.

Delete a Sales Agreement

Delete a Sales Agreement
  1. On the navigation bar, click Product Config and then click Sales Agreements.
  2. On the upper left, select a Product Owner (distributors only) in the drop-down list, and select a Product in the Product Agreement grid.

  3. To the right of the selected Product Agreement row in the grid, click the red X.

  4. Click Yes on the confirmation dialog to delete the sales agreement from the system.

Edit the Back Office Script for Submitting Applications

Edit the Back Office Script for Submitting Applications

Use the Edit Back Office Script feature to update the rule used to submit an application to the back office provider.

  1. On the navigation bar, click Product Config and then click Sales Agreements.

  2. Select a product in the Product Agreement grid.

  3. Click the Back Office Script Edit link.

  4. In the Back Office Window dialog, make the applicable changes to the Back Office Script.

    For assistance, click the Node Help link on the lower left of the dialog.

  5. Click the Save button to save the edits.

View Sales Agreements History

View Sales Agreements History

NOTE: See the History for Packages, Products, Sales Agreements, and Admin History Report Spotlight Feature Video for more information.

  1. On the navigation bar, click Product Config and then click Sales Agreements.

  2. Select a product in the Product Agreement grid.

  3. Click the Sales Agreement History tab.

  4. View the selected sales agreement history information in the applicable columns of the history grid: Action, Old Value, New Value, Action Date, and Login. In Utilities, Reports page, Admin Reports, run the Admin History Report for Sales Agreement History Audit Report to view an audit history report for one or all sales agreements during a selected time frame.
  5. In Utilities, Reports page, Admin Reports, run the Admin History Report for Sales Agreement Report to view a sales agreement audit history report that lists the Distributor Org, Product Name, Carrier Agreement, Distributor Agreement, State Approval, Assigned Packages, Selling Org, Firm Org, and Last Updated date for one or all sales agreements during a selected time frame.

NOTE: The system purges all sales agreement deployment and audit history that is more than one year old.

Manage Sales Agreement

Manage Sales Agreement

FireLight admin users can make updates to multiple Sales Agreements at one time. They can select multiple selling organizations at one time and have changes made (i.e., states, etc.) applied to all of the selected organizations. It should be noted that this feature does not allow for granular level updates (e.g., only update the states).

  1. On the navigation bar, click Product Config and then click Sales Agreements.

  2. Select a product in the Product Agreement grid.

  3. Below the Sales Agreement Details tab, click the Manage Sales Agreement button to open the Manage Sales Agreements dialog. In the list, the system displays an eye icon after organization names that have an existing selling agreement for the selected product.

    4. Note: You can select multiple Products at the same time.

  4. Create a new batch of sales agreements for the selected product: click the Rename link, enter a name in the Saved Batches text box, select the checkboxes for existing Selling Orgs in the list that is included in the batch, and click the Save Batch button.

    NOTE: It is possible for a selling organization to be saved to multiple batches.

  5. Rename a batch of sales agreements for the selected product: in the Saved Batches list, select a batch name and click the Rename link. Enter a name in the Saved Batches text box and click the Save Batch button. To cancel a rename, click the Cancel link to the right of the Saved Batches text box.

  6. Delete an existing batch for the selected product: in the Saved Batches list, select a batch name and click the Delete Batch button.

  7. Copy a sales agreement for the selected product: select checkboxes from the Products and Selling Orgs list and click the Copy Agreement button. On the Copy Confirmation dialog, click Yes to proceed with the copy process or click No to exit the copy process. NOTE: Copying a Sales Agreement will overwrite any existing agreements for the product with the selected organizations. If no Sales Agreement exists for a selected Organization, the system will create a new one for it. Upon success, the Manage Agreements dialog closes and the system displays a message indicating the number of Sales agreements that were copied on the lower right of the Sales Agreements page: "Sales Agreement successfully copied to x Selling Orgs."

  8. Delete sales agreements for selected products: select the checkboxes for Products and Selling Orgs with existing sales agreements and click the Delete Agreement button. On the Delete Confirmation dialog, click Yes to delete the sales agreements or click No to cancel the deletion. Upon success, the Manage Agreements dialog closes and the system displays a message indicating the number of Sales agreements that were deleted on the lower right of the Sales Agreements page: "Sales Agreement successfully deleted for x Selling Orgs." The system refreshes the Selling Org list on the main Sales Agreement screen will then be refreshed so the organizations with a deleted Sales Agreement for the product will no longer have an eye icon by their name.

Search for a Package Assigned to a Sales Agreement

Search for a Package Assigned to a Sales Agreement

This feature allows users to search for a package assigned to a sales agreement.

  1. Below the product grid, click Search Package in Sales Agreement. You can also search package names from the Available Packages and Assigned Packages text boxes by typing search text and then clicking the associated magnifying glass search icon.
  2. From the drop-down, select the desired package.

    3. The sales agreement to which the product is assigned along with the selling organization are listed with the selected sales agreement.
